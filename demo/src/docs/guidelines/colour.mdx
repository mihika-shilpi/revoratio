Black black doesn’t really exist (`SEE`: [Vantablack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vantablack), [Black 3.0](https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/shortcuts/2019/aug/05/black-30-anish-kapoor-and-the-art-worlds-pettiest-funniest-dispute)).
Moreover, hard blacks can ‘hurt’ on screen, whereas grey blacks look faded in print. Black is actually a pretty hard colour.
EIL has a different black for print and digital. Pure black is never used.
The print black is not a [rich black](https://printninja.com/printing-resource-center/printninja-file-setup-checklist/offset-printing-guidelines/offset-color-requirements/standard-black-vs-rich-black), but should always be used with [overprint](https://www.typografics.be/en/blog/overprint-what-and-why) on.

## Combinations

You are free to combine colours as you see fit for the most part, but here are some guidelines to prevent unfortunate results:

### Contrast

Especially when using text, be mindful of contrast. Web standards recommend, for AA (minimum) and AAA (high) the following contrasts: (use this tool to check)

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3421.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3421.png)

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3414.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3414.png)

The primary colours have been chosen such that EIL Black text always passes contrast checks on them.

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3416.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3416.png)

Avoid coloured text unless in EIL Sans Bold and adequately large.

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3415.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3415.png)

Avoid using coloured text on coloured backgrounds, even if it passes a contrast check.

### Pairing

Most creative surfaces are expected to have ONE main colour: other colour accents may be used, but let the creative be memorable for a single colour. That said, if combining colours:

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3417.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3417.png)

When placing colours next to each other, create energy in their contrast. ‘Bright’ is the brief.

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3418.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3418.png)

Do not place similar colours next to each other.

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3419.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3419.png)

Although this guidelines is met with exceptions, avoid in general, having two colour blocks touch each other. They are always separated by white space, or black outlines.

![Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3420.png](Colour%20b65fef1b4baf48b8b40ea5986c12809c/Group_3420.png)

Colours are never used directly atop each other without a white or black delineator.
